About

Thank you for helping me gather the data I need to complete my master's thesis! You'll find detailed instructions included with your download.

In the Western canon, we recognize that music usually adheres to some form of hierarchical structure. In a classical piece this might be the A-B-A sontata-allegro form. In pop music this could be the Verse-Chorus-Verse-Chorus-Bridge-Chorus-Chorus form. In the smaller extreme, short musical sequences such as hymn melodies or improvised jazz solos may carry repeated motives and phrases.

What machinery is needed to equip a computer with the ability to create new music, whether it be classical music, pop music or hymn music? I hypothesize that a creative system must 1) be able to understand music from a structural standpoint and 2) be capable of targeting a given (or generated) musical structure when it is composing.

There are a variety of algorithms that can find hierarchical patterns in generic sequences, and some may be more useful in a musical context than others. In order to evaluate and compare the performance of these algorithms, we seek a human baseline to establish ground truth structural interpretations. Your task will be to annotate several short excerpts of music. Please download a file, add your annotations, and upload them here when you're done.
